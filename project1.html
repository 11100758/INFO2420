Web Site Critiques

Evaluation Questions

  1. What was your first impression of this site?
  2. How does this site organize the flow of information? (Do they have links on the left and text on the right? Some other scheme? No scheme?)
  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?
  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?
  5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?
  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?
  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

Trader Joe's -- http://www.traderjoes.com/

1. My first impression of the site was that it was warm and fun. The big photo of tacos and the colorful layout made it look like a food magazine more than a grocery store website.

2. The site organizes its information with a navigation bar on the left side with icons for things like Products, Recipes, and Discover. The main content is in the center of the page.

3. The site structure mostly makes sense. The left sidebar navigation is a little confusing at first because it only shows icons without labels, but once you hover over them it becomes clearer.

4. The graphics and layout enhance the site. The food photos are high quality and the hand-drawn illustrations give it a unique and fun personality that matches the Trader Joe's brand.

5. The home page loaded in about 2-3 seconds which seems reasonable. The page started showing content quickly even before everything finished loading.

6. The text was easy to read. Dark text on a light background made everything clear. The headings used red and navy colors that stood out well without being hard to look at.

7. Yes, the site had a navigation sidebar on the left and a top bar with search and shopping list options. The navigation worked well overall but the icon-only sidebar could be confusing for new visitors.

8. Yes, the site used the same red, white, and navy color scheme on every page. The same fonts and illustration style also carried through the whole site which gave it a consistent look.

9. The site responds surprising well to small windows. The UI pretty much remains nearly unchanged, just more tightly packed but still user friendly.

10. The Fearless Flyer was something unusual and very well done. It is an online version of their print newsletter that reads like a magazine with fun stories about the products. Most grocery store websites do not have anything like this.
